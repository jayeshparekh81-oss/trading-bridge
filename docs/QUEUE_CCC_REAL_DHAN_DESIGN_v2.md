### 2.5 Rate-limit handling (Q5 APPROVED — expanded)

**Two-tier rate-limit isolation** between backfill (system) and on-demand (user-driven).

#### 2.5.1 Time-window separation (always on)

| Window (IST) | Backfill | On-demand (user) |
|---|---|---|
| **09:00 – 15:30 (market hours)** | **HALTED** — beat tasks no-op, script refuses to run. | Allowed, throttled to 20% of daily quota. |
| **15:30 – 16:00 (post-close)** | Allowed, 80% quota. | Allowed, 20% quota. |
| **16:00 – 09:00 (off-market)** | Allowed, 80% quota. | Allowed, 20% quota. |
| **Weekend (Sat 16:00 IST – Mon 09:00 IST)** | Allowed full 100% — no on-demand competition. | Allowed; quota irrelevant. |

`rate_limit_guard.py` checks the wall clock (IST) on every fetch:

- During market hours: backfill calls raise `BackfillSuspendedError` (logged, job marked `pending`, picked up next window).
- On-demand fetches always check the per-source quota counter.

#### 2.5.2 Daily quota split (when both share one Dhan account)

Dhan free tier: 100,000 calls/day per access-token.

| Source | Quota | Redis key |
|---|---|---|
| Backfill (system creds) | 80,000/day | `rate:dhan_historical:backfill:daily` |
| On-demand (user-driven) | 20,000/day shared across all users | `rate:dhan_historical:ondemand:daily` |

Per-user 5/sec key (`rate:dhan_historical:{user_id}`) **stays untouched** — that's the upstream physical cap, this is the budget cap.

#### 2.5.3 Kill switch (event-driven)

New flag: `BACKFILL_KILL_SWITCH` (Redis key, default `0`).

- Set to `1` by a new signal handler when **any live strategy fires** (paper or real). Handler hooks into the existing signal-fire publish path (read-only — no edits to `strategy_executor`; we subscribe to its existing Redis pub/sub channel).
- `rate_limit_guard.check()` reads the flag; if `1`, backfill calls suspend (job marked `paused_live_strategy`, no error raised).
- Auto-clears 5 minutes after the last signal fires (TTL refresh on each fire).
- Manual override: `python -m app.tasks.historical_backfill_tasks --release-killswitch`.

**Why event-driven and not just time-window:** off-hours backfill is fine *unless* a paper or live strategy is mid-execution (e.g., a paper session running outside market hours, or a delayed fill). Better to halt on the actual event than rely solely on the clock.

#### 2.5.4 Separate Dhan account (optional, recommended path)

If founder provisions a dedicated Dhan account for backfill:

- Full 100k/day for backfill alone.
- On-demand path keeps its own 100k/day.
- 5/sec per-token cap fully isolated.
- Env vars: `BACKFILL_DHAN_CLIENT_ID`, `BACKFILL_DHAN_ACCESS_TOKEN`, `BACKFILL_DHAN_REFRESH_TOKEN`.
- Time-window separation still applied as a defence-in-depth measure (during market hours backfill stays off — keeps backfill noise out of Dhan logs during the live trading window).

**Decision recorded:** Both code paths shipped. Single-account is the default; dual-account flips on when `BACKFILL_DHAN_CLIENT_ID` env is non-empty.

### 2.6 Idempotency

Two layers:

1. **Row-level:** PK on `(symbol, exchange, timeframe, timestamp)` + `INSERT ... ON CONFLICT DO NOTHING`.
2. **Job-level:** `historical_backfill_jobs` table (§4) — `(symbol, exchange, timeframe, window_start, window_end)` uniqueness. Worker skips a job that's already `status='succeeded'` within the freshness TTL.

### 2.7 JWT rotation mid-fetch

- Re-resolve credentials at the start of **each chunk**, not per job.
- On `BrokerAuthError` mid-fetch: pause the worker (no retry loop with stale token), publish `BROKER_DISCONNECTED`, send Hinglish admin email if system-user backfill.

### 2.8 Error + retry policy

| Error from `DhanHistoricalClient` | Per-chunk action | Per-job action |
|---|---|---|
| `BrokerAuthError` | Stop chunk loop. | `failed_auth`, do NOT retry. Notify. |
| `BrokerRateLimitError` (local) | Sleep `_LOCAL_RATE_RETRY_SLEEP_S * 2`, retry chunk once. | Job survives. |
| `BrokerRateLimitError` (upstream 429) | Already retried inside client. | Retry with backoff `[1m, 5m, 15m]`. |
| `BrokerUpstreamError` (5xx / non-JSON) | Re-raise. | Retry up to 3× over 1h. After 3: `failed_upstream`. |
| `BrokerInvalidParamsError` | Re-raise. | `failed_params` — do NOT retry. Symbol likely delisted. |
| Network timeout | Already retried inside client. | Retry identical to upstream. |
| `BackfillSuspendedError` (Q5 new) | N/A — caller raises before chunk runs. | Job stays `pending`. Beat scheduler retries next window. |
| `KillSwitchActiveError` (Q5 new) | N/A. | Job marked `paused_live_strategy`, beat retries after TTL. |

### 2.9 Backfill strategy

**Initial pull (per Q1 = A — full 507 symbols):**

| Timeframe | Window | Calls/symbol | Total calls | Wall-clock at 4 req/s (80% quota) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `1d` | 5 years | 1 | 507 | ~3 min |
| `1h` | 2 years (~6 × 90d chunks) | 6 | 3,042 | ~13 min |
| `5m` | 2 years | 8 | 4,056 | ~17 min |
| `1m` | DEFERRED to on-demand | — | — | — |

**Total ~33 min wall-clock for full backfill at 80% quota.** Comfortably fits one weekend Saturday night window.

- `5m / 15m / 1h`: weekday 16:00 IST → fetch today.
